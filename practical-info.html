Practical Information

The Program

The Language

Being it the international wedding, we are inviting you to follow our lifestyle and “meet in the midway”. In other words, we are keeping the international standard of communication and organize the wedding in English.

We are aware of the fact that some guests from both parts are not comfortable to speak or understand English, but we hope that you will still find a way to enjoy our special day. Maybe offer a drink for your neighbour that he/she would translate the parts you missed?

(Also, bring glasses if you have trouble reading from far as we will try that most parts would be translated on the screen )

For those that wish to make a toast and prefer to do it in their mother-tongue, we kindly ask to prepare it beforehand that it could be translated for the wedding. Please contact us and we will find a way to make it happen.

Accommodation

As it is common in Lithuania, though might be new to our overseas guests, we celebrate wedding all night long and the day after! Therefore, most of the wedding venues have places to sleep so that the next morning everyone can share breakfast and good stories together.

On the other hand, it is also common that the main attention is given to partying and sleeping is just a reward to your body after dancing all night.

In other words, the rooms in the venue are shared with several guests and are scattered throughout the area. While some of you will stay in the main house, others will enjoy a more pillow party experience, sharing the floor in a separate small house. While you may not expect a private en-suite, you can be certain to have a place for a rest, no matter how long you’ll party!

Dresscode

The general dress code for the wedding is black tie and the wedding colour is rose gold. While the particular dress code is discussed separately with our groomsmen and bridesmaids, we would appreciate other guests to wear even, pastel colour dresses and shirts, avoiding strong colours that doesn’t fit the palette.

It is important to notice, though, that the venue is in the nature, so steep high heels should be avoided for your own convenience (there will be walking on the grass and the entrance terrace is made of wooden floor with spaces).

In addition, as the overnight partying might suggest, it would be advisable to have spare, more casual clothes for the second day. As all the girls know the hell of putting high heels the next morning after party, less requiring clothes follow the same pattern. Thus, bring some extra clothes and shoes!

Alternative to Flowers

While in Brazil gifts are usually already given prior to the wedding, in Lithuania it is common to greet the newlyweds after the ceremony or before the reception. While the expression “one cannot come empty-handed” is applied everywhere, it is common to bring flowers to great the newlyweds.

As we already try to minimize the physical gifts, we would also like to ask to save the flowers and leave them to blossom in the fields. Though the beauty of them may, indeed, amaze us, we will not be able to enjoy them long as we cannot bring them home by plane.

Alternatively, we would like to suggest bringing something that may really enrich us for years to come. For those that wish to bring a physical gift, we would suggest to choose a book for our yet small home library. No surprise that Ricardo likes to read , but in these years around him even Egle grew interest in that ancient way of learning!

The only thing we would like to ask is to choose a book that is written in English so that we both can enjoy it!

The Next Day

After the breakfast and checking out of the rooms, we are planning to spend some hours relaxing by the lake. If the weather allows, you can enjoy swimming in the lake, rowing a boat or just laying around.

We will also organise some picnic food and barbeque so that we can all have an unofficial, yet as pleasurable celebration time by the lake.

Thus, we invite you not to hurry home after breakfast and spend the day relaxing.

Given that in the daylight nudity is not practised, don’t forget to bring your swimsuit!
